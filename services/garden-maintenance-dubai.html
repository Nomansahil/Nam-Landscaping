Overview

Professional Care, On a Schedule You Can Rely On

A Dubai garden left alone for a month in summer can take a season to recover. Consistent, knowledgeable care is the difference between a landscape that matures beautifully and one that quietly declines. Our gardeners follow a structured visit checklist tuned to the season, what your garden needs in February is not what it needs in August.

Every visit covers the essentials below, and every visit ends with a short WhatsApp report: photos, work completed and anything we recommend watching. If we spot an irrigation fault or pest problem early, you hear about it before it becomes expensive. Most clients combine garden care with pool maintenance under one annual contract.

Good to Know

Garden Maintenance: Your Questions

How much does garden maintenance cost in Dubai?

Garden maintenance with NAM starts from AED 240 per month for a small villa garden and AED 790 per month for a large one, charged as a fixed monthly fee. The exact figure depends on garden size, visit frequency and whether pool care is combined, confirmed after a free assessment.

How often should a Dubai garden be maintained?

Weekly visits suit most villa gardens, lawns grow fast under irrigation and heat. Smaller or low-planting gardens can work on bi-weekly visits. We recommend a frequency after seeing your garden, not before.

What does a maintenance visit include?

Mowing and edging, pruning, weeding, fertilising to schedule, pest checks, irrigation inspection and full clean-up, plus seasonal tasks like flower rotation and summer stress care. Every visit ends with a photo report.

Can you take over a garden another company installed?

Of course. We start with a free assessment, fix any inherited problems, and then maintain it as if we had built it ourselves.

Do you handle pest control and fertilisation?

Yes, both are built into the plan. We use targeted, garden-safe treatments and a feeding programme designed for Dubai's sandy, fast-draining soils.

What happens during the summer months?

Summer is when professional care matters most: watering schedules change, mowing heights rise and stress diseases appear. Our visits continue year-round with a summer-specific checklist.
